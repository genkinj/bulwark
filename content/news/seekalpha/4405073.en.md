Earnings Call Insights: Peloton Interactive (NASDAQ:[PTON](https://seekingalpha.com/symbol/PTON "Peloton Interactive, Inc.")) Q2 2025

### Management View

* Peter Stern, CEO, emphasized Peloton’s focus on innovation and cost optimization to empower members to live healthier lives. He highlighted the company's progress in improving unit economics, achieving double-digit Connected Fitness Products gross margins for the first time in three years, and exceeding $200M in annualized cost savings targets ahead of schedule.
* Stern noted that churn rates improved significantly for members engaging in multiple fitness disciplines, with churn 60% lower for members using two or more modalities. He also pointed out the success of the Tread portfolio, which exceeded sales goals and achieved higher subscription attachment rates year-over-year.
* CFO Liz Coddington reported, "We are pleased with our second quarter results, as we exceeded our guidance on key metrics and continued to make meaningful progress improving unit economics and profitability." She added that net debt decreased by over $280M, reflecting a 30% year-over-year reduction, and highlighted improvements in Adjusted EBITDA and Free Cash Flow by approximately $140M year-over-year.

### Outlook

* Peloton raised its full-year Fiscal 2025 adjusted EBITDA guidance by $60M to $300M-$350M, citing gross margin expansion and continued cost discipline. The company also increased its free cash flow target to at least $200M, up by $75M, reflecting operating efficiencies and inventory management improvements.
* Q3 Fiscal 2025 revenue guidance is set at $605M-$625M, reflecting seasonally lower hardware sales post-holiday. Total gross margin guidance for Q3 is projected at 50%, up 280 basis points sequentially due to a revenue mix shift favoring subscriptions.
* Fiscal 2025 revenue guidance was raised slightly to $2.43B-$2.48B, supported by higher Paid Connected Fitness Subscriptions and favorable Tread portfolio performance.

### Financial Results

* Total revenue for Q2 2025 reached $674M, exceeding guidance by $14M. Connected Fitness Products revenue was $253M, while Subscription revenue contributed $421M.
* Adjusted EBITDA came in at $58M, surpassing the guidance range by $28M. Free Cash Flow for Q2 was $106M, an improvement of $143M year-over-year.
* Gross margin increased to 47.2%, supported by double-digit Connected Fitness Products gross margins of 12.9%. Subscription gross margin was 67.9%, up 60 basis points year-over-year.
* Operating expenses decreased by $122M or 25% year-over-year, reflecting lower advertising spend, reduced personnel costs, and efficiency measures.

### Q&A

* **Simeon Siegel, BMO:** Asked about the timeline for achieving growth alongside profitability improvements. CEO Peter Stern responded that while the immediate focus is on rightsizing expenses and improving financial capacity, growth levers would include product innovation, meeting members in more places, and deepening member connections.
* **Shweta Khajuria, Wolfe Research:** Inquired about the benefits of deleveraging. CFO Liz Coddington highlighted a 50 basis point reduction in term loan rates due to improved leverage ratios, resulting in $5M annualized interest savings.
* **Curtis Nagle, Bank of America:** Asked about churn drivers. Stern emphasized low churn rates of 1.4%, supported by member engagement and satisfaction, while Coddington noted the positive impact of marketing efforts targeting reactivations.
* **Arpine Kocharyan, UBS:** Questioned potential price increases. Stern acknowledged the strategic importance of pricing adjustments, emphasizing careful consideration and recent learnings from reduced promotional activity.

### Sentiment Analysis

* Analysts expressed cautious optimism, focusing on profitability improvements and cost controls. Questions demonstrated interest in deleveraging, churn, and growth pathways. The tone was neutral to slightly positive, with analysts recognizing progress but seeking clarity on timelines for growth.
* Management remained confident in both prepared remarks and Q&A responses. CEO Peter Stern highlighted the company’s disciplined financial approach and focus on member outcomes, while CFO Liz Coddington detailed cost-saving measures and improved financial metrics.
* Compared to the previous quarter, management’s tone reflected increased confidence, supported by stronger financial results and revised guidance. Analysts maintained a consistent tone, pressing for details on growth and profitability balance.

### Quarter-over-Quarter Comparison

* Adjusted EBITDA guidance for FY 2025 increased by $60M in Q2, reflecting faster-than-expected cost savings and gross margin improvements. In Q1, the focus was on stabilizing the business and aligning costs; by Q2, management demonstrated tangible progress.
* Churn improved sequentially from 1.9% in Q1 to 1.4% in Q2, driven by higher member engagement and reactivation efforts.
* Analysts' focus shifted from stabilization in Q1 to growth potential in Q2, with greater emphasis on churn improvements and cost discipline.
* Management’s tone displayed greater confidence in Q2, supported by tangible financial achievements and upward revisions to guidance.

### Risks and Concerns

* Churn pressures from the secondary market remain a headwind, as subscriptions tied to used hardware exhibit higher churn rates.
* Potential tariff impacts on connected fitness products could affect profitability, though management stated the exposure is minimal.
* Macroeconomic uncertainties, including inflation and consumer spending trends, could weigh on hardware sales.
* Execution risks in achieving further cost optimizations and sustaining gross margin improvements.

### Final Takeaway

Peloton’s Q2 2025 earnings call highlighted significant progress in cost optimization, profitability, and financial discipline. Adjusted EBITDA guidance for FY 2025 was raised by $60M, reflecting gross margin expansion and operational efficiencies. Churn improved to 1.4%, supported by member engagement initiatives, while free cash flow hit $106M for the quarter. Management’s focus on innovation and meeting members in more places positions the company for future growth, though challenges remain in sustaining these trends amid macroeconomic uncertainties.
